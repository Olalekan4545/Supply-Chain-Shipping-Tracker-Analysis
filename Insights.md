- The SKU with the lowest stock level is *SKU 34* with *1* units remaining.
- Low stock levels indicate risk of stockouts and potential loss of sales.
- Restocking high-demand SKUs will improve product availability and Monitoring stock levels is essential to prevent product shortage and loss of sales.

## 5. Carrier Comparison
- The best performing carrier in terms of revenue is *Carrier B* with *0.25m* but also has higher shipping cost of *237*
- Carrier A and Carrier C are more cost-effective, but their revenue performance differs.
- choosing the most cost-effective carrier can improve profitability.

## 6. Key Opportunities
- Improve shipping quality for high-defect modes to reduce product losses.
- Optimize shipping routes and carriers to reduce cost.
- SKUs with low stock levels but high demand should be restocked to prevent stockouts, protect revenue, and maintain customer satisfaction.
- Low performing SKUs with low demand should be reviewed rather than restocked, using promotions or inventory reduction strategies.
- Reduce defect rates by improving packaging and handling for high-risk transportation modes.
- Focus on high revenue products to improve sales and profitability.
